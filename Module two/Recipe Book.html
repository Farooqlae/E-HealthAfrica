Jollof Rice

Jollof, or jollof rice, is a rice dish from West Africa. The dish is typically made with long-grain rice, tomatoes, onions, spices, vegetables and meat in a single pot, although its ingredients and preparation methods vary across different regions.

Ingredients Amount
Long grain Rice 1 cup
vegetable oil 1/2 cup
onions 1 medium, finely chopped
Diced Tomatoes 1 can
Chicken broth 1 cup
tomato paste 1 tablespoon
Cayenne pepper 1 tablespoon
ground thyme 1/4 teaspoon

Instructions:

1. Rinse the rice in a fine mesh strainer under cold running water until the water runs clear. Set aside.
2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
3. Add the tomatoes, chicken broth, tomato paste, cayenne pepper, thyme, and salt. Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes.
4. Add the rice to the saucepan and stir to combine. Add the peas and carrots. Cover the saucepan and let the rice cook over low heat for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through and the liquid has been absorbed.
5. Serve the jollof rice hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy!

Shito

Shito is a delicious hot pepper sauce prepared with dried fish and prawns along with onions, chilli, garlic, and some spices. This dark coloured Ghanaian sauce has a rich umami taste which just makes everything taste better. Ghana shito is a very versatile. In the local Ghanaian cuisine, it is used in ways similar to a chilli sauce or ketchup. It can be used as a side, dip or even dressing. It is traditionally eaten with kenkey, waakye, banku and many other Ghanaian dishes. However, it tastes amazing spread on bread, in a stir-fry or salad, stirred into pasta; on barbecue meat, fish or vegetables; anything really. This Ghanaian sauce is rich and aromatic, with intense flavours; so, a little of it goes a long way to enhance food. It is a good way to eat flavoursome food on the cheap. I was thinking back to my student days and how shito sauce would have been useful for when I was broke! If only I knew about it then.

Ingredients Amount
Red Pepper flakes 1 cup
garlic 1/2 cup
onions 1 medium, finely chopped
Tomatoe paste 1 can
ginger 1 cup
crayfish 1 tablespoon
Shrimp 1 tablespoon
ground chilli pepper 1/4 teaspoon

Instructions:

1. In a small saucepan, heat the dried prawns over low heat until they are fragrant and slightly toasted, about 5 minutes. Set aside.
2. In a large saucepan, heat the palm oil over medium heat. Add the onion, pepper, ginger, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onions are softened and the pepper is fragrant, about 5 minutes.
3. Add the tomatoes and cook until they are softened and beginning to break down, about 10 minutes.
4. Add the toasted prawns and crayfish, and stir to combine. Add the Maggi cubes and salt, and stir to combine.
5. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the oil begins to separate from the sauce.
6. Serve the shito hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy!
